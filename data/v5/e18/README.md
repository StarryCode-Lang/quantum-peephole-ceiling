## `e18_bias_report.txt` — content and whereabouts

**Whereabouts**: `derived/e18_bias_report.txt` (moved from this directory during
the 2026-07-20 data-integrity cleanup; generated by
`experiments/e18_bias_correction.py`).

**Content summary** (verbatim figures from the report):

1. *Missingness mechanism*: 270 total rows; 150 analyzable (55.6%); 78
   decompose-error (gate-set incompatibility, systematic); 42 fidelity-zero
   (width > max_qubits_fidelity=10, systematic).
2. *Formal missingness tests*: Little's MCAR d2 = 49.959, df = 1,
   p = 1.570e-12 → REJECT MCAR; classified MAR (explained by family + n_qubits).
3. *MAR assumption check*: within-family Levene tests — Adder p = 0.043 flagged;
   CNOT/Oracle/RandomClifford OK; GHZ/SurfaceCode NaN (flagged).
4. *Convertibility*: P(decomposable) = 71.1%; P(optimizable & verifiable) =
   55.6%; circuit-level failure fraction 64.8% (92/142).
5. *ITT analysis* (all 270 rows, 50 imputations): pooled ITT mean 0.1351
   [0.102, 0.169]; per-optimizer ITT — greedy 0.2781, commutation 0.0116,
   hybrid 0.2802.
6. *Survivor vs ITT*: pooled survivor mean 0.1800 is an **upper bound**;
   optimistic upper bound 0.1871; sensitivity bracket width 0.0520. Conclusion:
   report ITT-MI + sensitivity bracket in the manuscript, not the survivor
   subset.

## Survivorship bias caveat

- **Total trials**: 270
- **Failed trials**: 120 (78 `decompose_error` + 42 `fidelity=0`) — **44.4% failure rate**
- **Surviving trials**: 150

All quantitative claims in the manuscript that draw on E18 must carry the
annotation "(survivorship-biased; 44.4% failure rate)" or equivalent.

**Bias direction**: conservative. Circuits that fail Clifford+T decomposition
likely have *higher* structural complexity; their exclusion means the reported
mean reduction is likely an *overestimate* of the true population mean. The
bias direction is stated to prevent misinterpretation, not to dismiss the finding.

The ITT (intention-to-treat) analysis in `derived/e18_itt_report.csv` treats failed
trials as zero reduction and provides a lower-bound estimate. The imputation
draws in `derived/e18_imputation_draws.csv` (2100 rows = 270 trials × ~8 draws) provide
a multiple-imputation estimate under a missing-at-random assumption tested by
Little's MCAR (`derived/e18_littles_mcar.json`).
